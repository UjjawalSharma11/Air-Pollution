Air Pollution & India

In 2022, there was average PM2.5 concentration in India.
Which was 10.7 times the WHO annual air quality guidline value.

A Report by ORF

According to Observer Research Foundation, Air pollution in India accounted for 1.7 million premature deaths in 2019,
Which is nothing less than 17.8 percent of the total deaths recorded in the country that year.

Air pollution is a cross-sectoral problem with emissions originating from diverse sources.

  • 1). Industrial activities constitutes of 36% of total emissions.
  • 2). Residential combustion constitutes of 39% of total emissions.
  • 3). The transport sector, concentrated largely in urban centres, adds another 4 percent.
  • 4). Remaining 20% comes from miscellaneous sources like agricultural activities and construction works.

Steps Taken by GOI to Counter Air Pollution

According to SDG - 17, public and government partnership is very important to achieve our goal of healthier air.
The Central Government has launched National Clean Air Programme (NCAP) under the Central Sector “Control of Pollution” Scheme as a long-term, time-bound, national level strategy to tackle the air pollution problem across the country in a comprehensive manner.
With targets to achieve 20 % to 30 % reduction in PM10 and PM2.5 concentrations by 2024 keeping 2017 as the base year for the comparison of concentration.

Other important initiatives taken by GOI

  • 1). Compulsory PUC (Pollution under control) certification of period driven vehicles which test for carbon monoxide and hydrocarbons.
  • 2). Promotion of public transport network including Metros.
  • 3). A dedicated media corner, Twitter and Facebook accounts have been created for access to air quality related information and to provide a platform for lodging complaints by general population.
  • 4). For field feedback on air polluting activities in Delhi and major NCR towns, 46 teams of Central Pollution Control Board have been deployed since October 7, 2019.
